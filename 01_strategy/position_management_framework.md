# Position Management Framework

## Core Discipline: Stick to the Plan, Don't Chase Price

**Base principle:** Never chase price upward. Always let price come to your predetermined entry zones.

**Why this matters:**
- Chasing price = buying at highs = forced to exit at lows when thesis is still valid
- Sticking to plan = buying at lows = holding through upside with defined risk
- Reduced stress: If price drops after you miss, you'll have better entries waiting below instead of regret

### Two Operating Modes

**PASSIVE MODE (Default — Use most of the time):**
- Your 3 layers are set at 1st (4% below), 2nd (10% below), 3rd (16% below) — final and locked
- NEVER add layers above your original entry prices just because price went up
- NEVER increase entry prices upward to "stay in" a rallying stock
- Price will hit these levels eventually; if it doesn't, the thesis may have changed
- If a stock rallies hard past all 3 layers, wait for pullback to reload
- **This mode removes FOMO, reduces stress, and guarantees discipline**

**ACTIVE TRADING MODE (Exception — Earnings/catalyst weeks only):**
- OK to add layers at higher prices IF you are actively monitoring technicals in real-time
- Requires: fast reaction times, ability to exit quickly if setup breaks, multiple daily reviews
- Treat higher-layer additions as intraday momentum trades, not core positions
- Risk is lower because you can respond immediately to invalidation signals
- Use ONLY during: earnings season, macro catalyst weeks, or high-volatility setups
- Switch back to passive mode when catalyst passes or you lose monitoring capacity

**Key distinction:** Passive mode = peace of mind + discipline. Active mode = tactical flexibility, but ONLY if you're actually watching. Never attempt active mode while distracted.

## When NOT to Trade: Emotional State Risk Management

**If you're sick, too tired, stressed, or on holiday: CLOSE or TRIM all positions.**

**Why:** Unstable emotional state = poor decision-making = forced exits at bad times. Tired/stressed traders exit winners early, hold losers too long, and miss key signals.

**Action checklist:**
- **Before long holiday:** Close/trim to 0 positions. Cancel all pending 1st layer orders if you won't be monitoring.
- **If sick >3 days:** Close 1st/2nd layers; consider closing 3rd layer if thesis still uncertain.
- **If chronically stressed/tired:** Trim 50% of holdings; reduce active tickers from 4 to 2 max.
- **During recovery:** Don't re-enter until you feel stable for 2+ consecutive days.

**Exception:** Pending mode (1st layer limit orders only) can stay live if you cancel them before travel.

**Remember:** Missing a trade while healthy > making a bad trade while compromised. Your layers will still be there after recovery.

## Position Structure

### Portfolio Constraints

- **Active holdings:** 4 tickers max (fully deployed with 2-3 layers filled)
- **Sector concentration:** Max 2 sectors in active portfolio
  - Example: Can have 2 gold miners + 2 oil stocks, but not 1 miner + 1 oil + 1 tech + 1 auto
- **Per-ticker position size:**
  - **Standard mode:** $20,000 per ticker (normal market conditions)
  - **Aggressive/Active mode:** Can exceed $20k significantly when conditions align (see below)
- **Pending positions:** Up to 10 tickers with 1st layer buy orders only (low capital at risk)
  - Waiting for cheaper entry prices
  - Executed during low-volume periods (pre-market, after-hours, overnight)
  - Reviewed when price hits the limit order OR macro/catalyst changes thesis

### Aggressive Position Sizing (When to Exceed $20k per Ticker)

Push position size MUCH larger in these high-conviction scenarios:

**Trigger 1: Post-Earnings Setup (Price Lags Fundamentals)**
- Earnings beat or raised guidance released
- Stock price hasn't caught up yet (still lagging move)
- Your research validates the upside thesis
- Action: Significantly increase 2nd/3rd layer sizes (300-500+ shares each)
- Example: XPEV earnings beat with margin expansion → 1st @ 100, 2nd @ 500, 3rd @ 500 = $1,600 + $7,500 + $7,000 = **$16,100**

**Trigger 2: Sector Rotation (One Name Lags Peer Strength)**
- Sector rally is strong (e.g., gold miners up 6%+)
- One name in the sector significantly lags peers
- Your research shows it should be performing as well or better
- Conviction is high that it will catch up
- Action: Aggressively size into the lagging name
- Example: Gold sector +6%, but one miner only +1% while peers +6% → push position to $25-30k if conviction supports

**Trigger 3: Technical Confirmation + Fundamental Setup Aligns**
- Earnings/guidance is strong (fundamental)
- Price breaks above resistance with high volume (technical)
- Your conviction from research is validated by price action
- Action: Scale aggressively to capture momentum
- Example: After earnings breakout, double down on 3rd layer sizing

**Position Sizing in Aggressive Mode:**
- Still use 1st layer @ 100 shares (IBKR visibility)
- 2nd/3rd layers can be 300-500+ shares each
- Total position can reach $25,000 - $40,000+ if conviction and setup warrant
- Still respect the 4-active-holdings limit and 2-sector max
- Exit discipline remains critical: take profits on upper layers more aggressively

### Transition: Pending → Active

- **Trigger:** 1st layer entry price is filled at market (100 shares)
- **Action step 1 - Conviction review:** After fill, assess thesis:
  - Did the fill happen as expected? At entry price or better?
  - Has macro/catalyst changed since setting the 1st layer?
  - Do you want to add more exposure on pullbacks (2nd/3rd)?
- **Action step 2 - Size 2nd/3rd layers:** Based on conviction:
  - High conviction → 2nd layer 150-200 shares @ $15.90, 3rd layer 150-200 shares @ $15.70
  - Medium conviction → 2nd layer 100 shares @ $15.90, 3rd layer 100 shares @ $15.70
  - Low conviction → 2nd layer 50 shares @ $15.90, skip 3rd or 50 shares only
- **Action step 3 - Activate orders:** Place 2nd/3rd layer buy orders at determined sizes
- **Update tracking:** Add to daily review checklist and holdings CSV
- **Example:** XPEV 1st layer @ $16.00 fills → review conviction (high) → size 2nd @ 200 sh, 3rd @ 200 sh → activate orders

### Standard Setup (Fully Active Position)
- **Max per ticker:** $20,000 total capital
- **Layer sizing:**
  - **1st layer:** 100 shares (fixed) — only size visible off-hours on IBKR
  - **2nd layer:** 100+ shares (adjustable per conviction review after 1st fills)
  - **3rd layer:** 100+ shares (adjustable per conviction review after 2nd fills)
- **Total exposure:** Starts at 300 shares (baseline), scales up to $20k max depending on conviction
- **IBKR technical note:** 
  - Below 100 shares don't show in IBKR pre-market/after-hours quotes
  - 100 shares is the minimum visible lot size off-hours
  - Larger sizes also visible but may move less liquidity in pre-market

### Layer Size Adjustments (Post-1st-Layer Fill)

After 1st layer fills at market, **review conviction** before setting 2nd/3rd layer sizes:

**Standard Conviction Levels:**
- **High conviction** (thesis validated by entry + upcoming catalyst strong):
  - 2nd layer: 400 shares @ $15.00 = $6,000
  - 3rd layer: 400 shares @ $14.00 = $5,600
  - **Total position:** $1,600 + $6,000 + $5,600 = **$13,200**
- **Medium conviction** (thesis on track but some uncertainty):
  - 2nd layer: 100 shares @ $15.00 = $1,500
  - 3rd layer: 100 shares @ $14.00 = $1,400
  - **Total position:** $1,600 + $1,500 + $1,400 = **$4,500**
- **Lower conviction** (need more data before committing):
  - 2nd layer: 50 shares @ $15.00 = $750
  - 3rd layer: skip
  - **Total position:** $1,600 + $750 = **$2,350**

**Aggressive Conviction (Post-Earnings Lag / Sector Rotation):**
- **Scenario: Earnings beat + price significantly lags**
  - 2nd layer: 500-600 shares @ $15.00 = $7,500-9,000
  - 3rd layer: 500-600 shares @ $14.00 = $7,000-8,400
  - **Total position:** $1,600 + $7,500-9,000 + $7,000-8,400 = **$16,100-18,400**
- **Scenario: Sector +6%, this name only +1%, conviction to catch up**
  - 2nd layer: 400 shares @ $15.00 = $6,000
  - 3rd layer: 400 shares @ $14.00 = $5,600
  - **Total position:** $1,600 + $6,000 + $5,600 = **$13,200** (or scale to $25-30k if extra conviction)

**Timing:** Adjust layer sizes AFTER 1st fills, not before. This lets you review the actual fill price and thesis validation before scaling. Aggressive sizing only justified by clear catalysts (earnings, sector rotation, peer lagging).

## Short-Term Gap Trading Strategy

### When & Why Gap Trading Works

**Pre-market / After-hours gaps (>6%) are opportunities for quick swing trades:**

- **Gaps happen on:** Earnings surprises, macro shocks, geopolitical events, major guidance changes
- **Why they fill:** Institutional traders and market-makers rebalance during RTH (regular trading hours)
- **Your edge:** Off-hours quotes have lower liquidity, so gaps tend to be wider than they "should" be
- **Execution window:** Fill gap dips in pre-market (Jakarta afternoon/evening), sell into RTH strength at market open (Jakarta evening/night)
- **Jakarta timezone note:** US market open (9:30 AM EDT) = **8:30 PM Jakarta same day**; Pre-market (4:00 AM EDT) = **3:00 PM Jakarta same day**

### Gap Trading Rules

| Rule | Logic | Action |
|------|-------|--------|
| **Gap size threshold** | Only trade gaps >6% (worth the execution risk) | Ignore gaps <6%; stick to longer-term layering strategy |
| **Entry timing** | Buy the dip in pre-market (lower prices due to lower liquidity) | Monitor pre-market (4:00-9:30 AM ET) for large gaps; place buy orders on the dip |
| **Entry sizing** | Keep position sizes smaller than long-term holdings (faster in/out) | Use 100-200 shares per gap trade (not 300-500+ like aggressive mode) |
| **Exit target** | Sell 3-4% above entry (capture quick bounce) | Place sell order immediately after buy fills; most gaps fill within 30-60 min of market open |
| **Max hold time** | Exit by market open + 1 hour OR get stopped out | Do NOT hold gap trades beyond 1-2 hours into RTH; miss the initial gap-fill and exit |
| **Stop loss** | If gap doesn't bounce 3-4%, bail (don't average down) | Set mental stop at entry price or -2%; exit if gaps reverses instead of fills |

### Gap Trading Execution Example

**Scenario: XPEV earnings miss, pre-market gap down 8%**
- Reference price yesterday: $16.00
- Pre-market gap: $16.00 × (1 - 0.08) = **$14.72**
- Action (all times Jakarta):
  - **3:30 PM (4:30 AM EDT):** Monitor pre-market, see -8% gap forming
  - **4:00 PM (5:00 AM EDT):** Price settles around $14.80 (some early dip-buying)
  - **4:15 PM (5:15 AM EDT):** Place BUY order 100 shares @ $14.72 (bottom of gap)
  - **4:30 PM (5:30 AM EDT):** Order fills @ $14.75 (got near-dip price)
  - **4:35 PM (5:35 AM EDT):** Place SELL order 100 shares @ $15.25 (3.4% above fill)
  - **8:30 PM (Market Open, 9:30 AM EDT):** Sell order executes @ $15.15-15.35 (gap is filling)
  - **Result:** Bought @ $14.75, sold @ $15.18, gain = **+2.9%** in 4 hours with minimal capital at risk

### Gap Trading vs. Long-Term Layering

| Aspect | Gap Trading | Long-Term Layering |
|--------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Time frame** | 30 min - 2 hours | Days to weeks |
| **Capital per trade** | $1,500-3,000 (100-200 shares) | $4,500-16,100+ |
| **Entry trigger** | Pre-market gap >6% | Price hits 1st layer at predetermined level |
| **Exit discipline** | Fixed 3-4% profit target or stop at -2% | Layers exited on thesis validation (earnings, sector) |
| **Market open behavior** | Exit before/at market open | Hold through open, often scale more aggressively |
| **Risk profile** | Quick in/out, gap-fill execution risk | Longer hold, thesis/catalyst risk |
| **Use case** | Cash neutral during regular day trading | Capital deployment for longer convictions |

### Position Sizing & Portfolio Impact

- **Gap trades are SEPARATE from your 4-active holdings**
  - Don't count toward the 4-active limit
  - Use "dry powder" or rotation capital
  - Exit completely by market open + 1-2 hours
- **Sizing:** Keep each gap trade to 100-200 shares (stay liquid, minimize market impact)
- **Frequency:** Only when gap >6% appears (typically 1-3 times per week depending on earnings season)
- **Portfolio integration:**
  - Gap trade profits are "free" swing money, not core positions
  - Can rotate gap trade capital into 1st layer pending orders if thesis validates post-earnings
  - Example: Gap down on XPEV earnings miss, sell gap trade at +2.9%, then start building layered position if research still bullish

### Pre-Market Monitoring Checklist

**Daily (3:00 PM - 8:30 PM Jakarta, before market open):**

1. **Scan for large gaps (>6%) — 3:00 PM - 4:30 PM Jakarta:**
   - Check your watchlist tickers for pre-market moves
   - IBKR shows pre-market bid/ask starting 3:00 PM Jakarta (4:00 AM ET)
   - Filter for earnings releases or macro shocks overnight (US overnight = Jakarta afternoon)

2. **Identify the "dip buy" level — 4:00 PM - 5:00 PM Jakarta:**
   - Gap size = (pre-market price - yesterday close) / yesterday close
   - If -8% gap, buy near the worst of the dip (usually 5-10 min after news breaks)
   - Don't chase the dip if price is already bouncing back; wait for stabilization

3. **Size and set orders — 4:15 PM - 5:30 PM Jakarta:**
   - BUY: 100-200 shares at dip price
   - SELL: Same shares at entry + 3-4%
   - Place both orders at same time (buy limit + sell limit once buy fills)

4. **Monitor fill & exit — 8:00 PM - 10:00 PM Jakarta:**
   - Pre-market fills are common (low volume but real executions)
   - Watch for bounce toward exit target during 8:00-8:30 PM (final pre-market hour)
   - Most fills happen within first 15-30 minutes of 8:30 PM market open
   - Exit NO LATER than 9:30 PM Jakarta (10:30 AM EDT RTH is "too late" for gap trades)

### When NOT to Gap Trade

**Skip gap trading if:**
- Gap is <6% (profit margin too tight after commissions/slippage)
- Thesis on the company has fundamentally broken (e.g., bankruptcy news, guidance disaster)
- Your conviction is actually bullish but news makes you want to flip it (conflict of interest — stick to long thesis instead)
- You don't have time to monitor pre-market and market open (gap trades need active management)
- Your active holdings are already at 4 tickers (no brain space for another trade)

### Gap Trade Exit Discipline

**Non-negotiable rules:**

1. **Exit by 10:30 AM RTH minimum** (latest: 11:00 AM)
   - Gap fills happen fast; if still open by 10:30, the gap probably isn't filling
   - Exit and lock in profit or take small loss, don't hold longer

2. **If sell order doesn't hit by market open +30 min, manually check:**
   - Is gap still alive or has it filled?
   - Is price stalling below your exit target?
   - If stalling, take the bid (market sell) at 2-3% profit instead of waiting for limit

3. **Stop loss at -2% or break-even:**
   - If gap reverses sharply instead of filling (e.g., news retraced), exit immediately
   - Don't average down or "wait for the bounce"; take the small loss and move on

## Managing Pending Positions (1st Layer Watchlist)

### Why Pending Positions Matter

Pending positions are your **opportunity queue** — you have 10 tickers with 1st layer buy orders sitting at predetermined prices, waiting for price to reach them. This is **capital-efficient** because:

- Only 1st layer is live (low capital deployed until fill)
- Avoids FOMO of chasing; lets price come to you
- Captures off-hours volatility (pre-market, after-hours, gaps)
- Keeps you disciplined: only add tickers with clear theses

### Pending Position Rules

| Rule | Why | Action |
|------|-----|--------|
| **Max 10 pending** | Prevents analysis paralysis; keeps watchlist manageable | Monitor 10 limit orders daily; once filled, move to active |
| **1st layer FIXED at 100 shares** | IBKR only displays 100-share batch sizes off-hours; sizes below 100 don't show, sizes above 100 may show but less reliable | Always use 100 shares for 1st layer pending orders |
| **Off-hours fills OK** | Price often overshoots at volume troughs; IBKR pre-market/after-hours captures these moves | Check pre-market, after-hours, and overnight for fills |
| **Review conviction after 1st fills** | Once 1st layer fills, re-assess thesis before sizing 2nd/3rd | Determine if high/medium/low conviction, then set 2nd/3rd layer sizes accordingly (100-200 shares) |
| **No adding layers while pending** | Avoid confusion; treat pending and active as separate | Once 1st fills → review conviction → activate 2nd/3rd at appropriate sizes; do NOT layer-add into pending |

### Daily Pending Position Check

- **Morning (before RTH):** Did any 1st layer fill overnight or pre-market? If yes:
  - Move ticker to active portfolio
  - Activate 2nd and 3rd layer orders
  - Add to daily monitoring checklist
- **During market:** Monitor for additional fills or catalyst changes
- **After RTH:** Check after-hours for volume spikes or gap setups

### Canceling Pending Positions

Cancel a 1st layer limit order if:
- Thesis breaks (earnings miss, guidance cut, sector rotation)
- Macro environment shifts materially (e.g., sharp DXY spike against the thesis)
- Price moves materially above 1st layer AND thesis is not stronger (avoid chasing)
- 4 active positions are full and you want to rotate into a different pending ticker

## Entry Pricing Logic

### How to Set the 3 Entry Prices

Use a **mixed approach** combining technical support, percentage pullback, and fundamental valuation:

1. **Identify the reference price** (current or recent market price)
   - Source: Watchlist `reference_price` or today's closing price
   - Example: XPEV at $6.70 (hypothetical reference)

2. **1st layer (Opportunistic entry)**
   - ~4% below reference
   - Captures initial pullback
   - Example: XPEV @ $16 (≈4% below $16.68 reference)

3. **2nd layer (Moderate pullback)**
   - ~10% below reference
   - Adds on larger dip, still in reasonable valuation range
   - Example: XPEV @ $15 (≈10% below reference)

4. **3rd layer (Core conviction)**
   - ~16% below reference
   - Long-term holding layer, significant margin of safety
   - Example: XPEV @ $14 (≈16% below reference)

**Refine with:**
- Prior support/resistance levels (technical analysis)
- 52-week low/high context
- Peer valuations if sector-relevant
- Macro headwinds (weakness in DXY = bullish for EM, rising rates = bearish for growth stocks)

## Exit Rules (Partial & Full)

### Layer-by-Layer Exit Strategy

| Layer | Entry Logic | Exit Trigger | Exit Price (Formula) | Purpose |
|-------|-------------|--------------|----------------------|---------|
| **1st** | Opportunistic dip | Spike + margin of safety | Entry × 1.00 to 1.02 | Lock minimum gains, test thesis |
| **2nd** | Moderate pullback | Bounce + catalyst risk | Entry × 1.05 to 1.08 | Trim opportunistically, reduce risk |
| **3rd** | Core conviction | Long-term target or reset | Entry × 3.0+ OR price-based reset | Extended upside capture |

### Exit Decision Framework

**For 1st & 2nd Layers (Tactical Exits):**
1. Has the stock bounced off the entry level? ✓
2. Is there upcoming catalyst risk (earnings, macro, product)? ✓ → Consider reducing
3. Is the bounce touching a technical resistance level? ✓ → Set sell order there
4. Calculate gain: `(exit_price - entry_price) / entry_price`
   - If gain ≥ 1-2% on 1st layer → execute (lock in margin of safety)
   - If gain ≥ 5-8% on 2nd layer → consider execution (especially if catalyst risk)

**For 3rd Layer (Core Position):**
1. Is the target price on the horizon? (e.g., XPEV $17+)
2. Is there a time-bound catalyst upcoming that could derail the thesis?
3. Has macro fundamentally shifted against the position? (e.g., DXY spike, rate hike)
4. **Hold unless:**
   - Price hits target → trim/exit based on conviction update
   - Thesis breaks (e.g., earnings miss + guidance cut) → reassess
   - Catalyst event changes outlook → reassess

## Price-Based Layer Reset

**When does a position reset into new layers?**

If the 3rd layer doesn't hit the target and price **drops below the 3rd layer entry price**, treat it as a **new setup:**

1. **Reset condition met:** Price drops to ≤ $5.70 (example: XPEV 3rd layer)
2. **Action:**
   - Reassess the thesis (earnings? Macro? Guidance?)
   - If thesis still valid, set NEW 3 layers at lower prices (e.g., $5.50, $5.30, $5.10)
   - If thesis broken, exit or move to watchlist pending reassessment
3. **Do NOT** add unlimited 4th, 5th, 6th layers — max 3 active at once
4. **Do NOT** average down indefinitely if the stock keeps falling

## Special Rules for Catalyst Events

### Pre-Earnings (1-3 weeks before)
- **2nd layer:** Consider tightening exit targets (catalyst risk)
  - May sell earlier than planned to avoid post-earnings gap
  - Example: Sell 2nd layer if it bounces 5-6% instead of waiting for 8%
- **3rd layer:** Hold through earnings ONLY if:
  - Conviction is high (strong technical setup + supportive macro)
  - Earnings is likely a beat or in-line (not a miss)
  - Post-earnings upside target is compelling (e.g., $17+ on XPEV)

### Post-Earnings
- **If miss + guidance cut:** Exit 3rd layer or reset lower
- **If beat + raised guidance:** Hold 3rd layer for extended target
- **If in-line:** Reassess thesis vs macro; may hold or trim

### Macro Catalysts (NFP, CPI, Fed decision, DXY spike)
- **Precious metals miners:** Exit upper layers on NFP miss (negative for gold), hold core on beat
- **Energy stocks:** Exit upper layers on supply concerns (bullish for oil), hold core on demand weakening
- **China EV (XPEV, NIO, LI):** Exit upper layers on strong DXY / rate hikes, hold core on China stimulus

## Position Sizing Guidelines

### Capital Allocation per Ticker

**Standard Mode ($20k per ticker):**
- **Base structure (low conviction):** 100 + 100 + 100 = 300 shares = $4,500
- **Medium conviction:** 100 + 100 + 100 = 300 shares = $4,500
- **High conviction:** 100 + 400 + 400 = 900 shares = **$13,200**

**Aggressive Mode (Post-Earnings / Lagging Name / High Conviction):**
- **Example XPEV (Post-Earnings Beat, Price Lagging):**
  - 1st layer: 100 shares @ $16.00 = $1,600 (already filled)
  - 2nd layer: 500 shares @ $15.00 = $7,500 (aggressive size)
  - 3rd layer: 500 shares @ $14.00 = $7,000 (aggressive size)
  - **Total capital deployed:** $16,100 (well-justified post-earnings setup)
- **Example Gold Miner (Sector +6%, This Name +1%, High Conviction Catch-Up):**
  - 1st layer: 100 shares @ $35.00 = $3,500 (already filled)
  - 2nd layer: 400 shares @ $33.00 = $13,200 (sector rotation sizing)
  - 3rd layer: 400 shares @ $31.00 = $12,400 (peer catch-up conviction)
  - **Total capital deployed:** $29,100 (justified by peer lagging + research conviction)

### When to Adjust Layer Size
- **Increase layer size (>100 shares):**
  - During active trading period (earnings/macro catalyst expected)
  - If conviction is extremely high and thesis validated
  - If macro setup is unusually supportive
  - Example: Use 400 shares/layer on high conviction (scales to $1,600 + $6,000 + $5,600 = $13,200)
  - Can scale to $20k max if multiple high-conviction positions warrant full commitment
- **Decrease layer size (<100 shares):**
  - If conviction drops (earnings/guidance disappointing)
  - If macro backdrop turns hostile (rising rates, strong DXY vs commodities)
  - If thesis becomes uncertain pending catalyst
  - Example: Use 50 shares/layer if on watchlist awaiting re-analysis before committing capital

## Review Cadence

### Daily (All Active + Pending Positions + Gap Trades)

**Pre-Market (4:30 AM - 9:30 AM ET):**
- **Scan for gap opportunities (>6%):** Monitor your watchlist for large pre-market moves
  - Earnings announcements overnight?
  - Macro shocks (Fed, geopolitical)?
  - Large gaps = potential quick swing trades
- **Execute gap trades if setup is clean:**
  - Buy dip in pre-market
  - Set sell order at +3-4%
  - Monitor for fill at market open
- **Check pending orders:** Did any 1st layers fill overnight?
  - If filled: Move to active, activate 2nd/3rd layers

**During RTH (9:30 AM - 4:00 PM ET):**
- **Gap trades:** Exit by 10:30-11:00 AM (lock profit or cut loss)
- **Active positions:** Monitor price action and exit targets
- **Review macro calendar:** Any releases today that impact sectors?

**Post-RTH & After-Hours (4:00 PM - 8:00 PM ET):**
- **Gap trade summary:** Log successful trades and lessons learned
- **Check pending 1st layers:** Any fills on pending orders?
- **Review sector rotation:** Did today's moves change your conviction on any pending tickers?

### Weekly (Before Weekly Review)

**Active holdings:**
- Summarize layer status (which are filled, which pending exits)
- Check if 3rd layer is approaching target
- Review upcoming catalysts next week

**Pending positions:**
- Are there pending 1st layers that should be cancelled? (thesis changed, macro shifted)
- Which pending ticker is closest to filling? (potential activation next week)
- Review sector allocation: Are pending tickers all in same sector? (diversify if planning rotations)

### Post-Catalyst (Immediate)

- If earnings released, compare results to thesis
- If macro data released, check impact on sector
- Reassess active 3rd layer target or reset if thesis changed
- Check if any pending positions need cancellation due to new data

### Pre-Catalyst (1-3 Days Before)

- Confirm active entry prices are still valid
- Tighten active 2nd layer exit if catalyst risk is high
- Confirm active 3rd layer holding conviction
- Review pending positions for pre-catalyst fills (low volume before big events)

## Portfolio Construction Rules

### Active vs. Pending Tickers

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                    OVERALL PORTFOLIO                     │
├────────────────────────┬────────────────────────────────┤
│   ACTIVE (4 tickers)   │  PENDING (10 tickers)          │
│   2 sectors max        │  Waiting for 1st layer fills   │
├────────────────────────┼────────────────────────────────┤
│ • Full 3 layers        │ • 1st layer order only         │
│ • Daily monitoring     │ • Off-hours entry possible     │
│ • Daily P&L            │ • Low capital at risk          │
│ • Sector allocation    │ • Reviewed when price hits     │
│ • Thesis updates       │ • Cancelled if thesis breaks   │
└────────────────────────┴────────────────────────────────┘
```

### When to Rotate Between Active & Pending

**Add pending → active:**
- 1st layer fills at market → move ticker to active, activate 2nd/3rd layers

**Remove active → archive:**
- Exit 3rd layer (thesis complete or broken)
- Close all layers before rotating to new pending candidate

**Do NOT:**
- Have more than 4 active (fully deployed) at once
- Have more than 2 sectors in active holdings
- Add a 4th layer while pending (strict 3-layer max per active ticker)

## Decision Checklist Before Opening a New Position

| Step | Check | Decision |
|------|-------|----------|
| 1 | **Thesis clear?** | What is the bull case and time horizon? (6 weeks? 6 months?) |
| 2 | **Macro supportive?** | DXY, yields, sentiment — do they support this bet? |
| 3 | **Catalyst identified?** | Earnings, product launch, macro event — what is the price driver? |
| 4 | **Entry prices set?** | 1st layer @ 100 shares; 2nd/3rd sizing TBD after conviction review |
| 5 | **Target price realistic?** | Is 3x upside (e.g., $14 → $42+) based on valuation or just hope? |
| 6 | **Risk defined?** | If thesis breaks, where is the stop? (price reset trigger) |
| 7 | **Conviction level?** | Low/Medium/High standard, or Aggressive (post-earnings lag, peer lagging, sector rotation)? |
| 8 | **Aggressive sizing justified?** | If scaling >$15k per ticker, confirm: post-earnings beat? Peer lagging but sector strong? Your research high conviction? |

## When to Escalate or Exit Entirely

**Thesis invalidated if:**
- Earnings show production declining (not just revenue)
- Guidance lowered 2+ consecutive quarters
- Management change or strategic shift contradicts original thesis
- Macro backdrop fundamentally shifts (e.g., tech fund outflows, rising rates)
- Stock breaks below 3rd layer AND macro is turning worse

**Action:** Exit 3rd layer immediately or move to watchlist for 30-day reassessment before re-entry.

## Integration with CSV Tracking

### Watchlist CSV (`04_portfolio/watchlist/watchlist.csv`)

Track **both active and pending** tickers here:

| Status Value | Meaning | Layer Status | Action |
|--------------|---------|--------------|--------|
| `Pending - 1st Layer` | Waiting for entry | 1st layer (100 sh) order live | Monitor daily for fill; review conviction when fills; cancel if thesis breaks |
| `Active - 1st Filled` | 1st filled, reviewing conviction | 1st layer filled (100 sh), 2nd/3rd pending | Assess thesis, size 2nd/3rd based on conviction (100-200 sh each), activate orders |
| `Active - 2 Layers Filled` | 1st + 2nd filled, 3rd pending | 1st + 2nd filled, 3rd layer order live | Monitor 3rd entry; confirm 2nd exit targets if catalyst risk rising |
| `Active - 3 Layers Filled` | Full position deployed | All 3 layers live | Monitor exits per catalyst; manage 3rd layer to target |
| `Hold` | Position complete | All layers exited except 3rd | Holding core for extended target |
| `Archive` | Thesis broken/completed | Exited | Move to archive after decision log |

**Columns to maintain:**
- `status`: Use values above
- `current_price`: Refresh daily
- `current_price_timestamp`: Jakarta time (YYYY-MM-DD HH:MM:SS)
- `target_buy_zone`: The 3 layer prices (e.g., "6.12 / 5.90 / 5.70")
- `thesis_summary`: Include layer status and current target
- `next_review_scheduled_at`: 1-3 days before earnings or macro event

