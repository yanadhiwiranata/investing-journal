## 1. SILVER MINER MACRO REGIME

Silver is a **hybrid commodity**: ~50% industrial demand + ~50% safe-haven/inflation hedge. **This makes silver miners 2–3x more volatile than gold miners.**

### Entry Regime Analysis

| Regime | Silver Price | Industrial Cycle | Risk Sentiment | Macro Setup | Entry Signal |
|--------|--------------|------------------|----------------|------------|--------------|
| **SILVER BULL (Industrial+)** | Breaking higher | Strong (ISM PMI >50) | Risk-on | Growth + inflation | ✅ **ACTIVATE** (best) |
| **SILVER SAFE HAVEN** | Spiking >3% | Weakening | Flight-to-safety | Geopolitical shock | ⚠️ **CAUTION** (fade quickly) |
| **SILVER STABLE** | Range-bound $74–$80 | Moderate (ISM 50–52) | Mixed | Sideways | ⏸ **PENDING ONLY** |
| **SILVER CORRECTION** | Falling >3% | Weakening (ISM <50) | Risk-off | Growth fears | ❌ **AVOID new entries** |
| **SILVER COLLAPSE** | Breaking below $70 | Recession signal | Panic selling | Deflationary shock | ❌ **EXIT immediately** |

**Silver Miner Leverage (2.5x–3.0x):**
- Silver +2% → Silver miner (AG, EXK) typically +5–6% (asymmetric upside)
- Silver –2% → Silver miner typically –6–7% (asymmetric downside — dangerous)
- **Implication:** Smaller position sizes justified; volatility is 2–3x worse than gold miners

## 3. LAYER-BASED ENTRY SYSTEM FOR SILVER MINERS

### Layer 1: Foundation Entry (Smaller Size for Volatility)

**Price Calculation: Different than Gold**

Silver miners require a **deeper discount** because volatility is higher.

```
Layer 1 Target = (Average PT from 5 latest analysts) × (0.90 to 0.93)

Example:
- Analyst consensus PT: $65 (AG = Hecla)
- Layer 1 target: $65 × 0.91 = $59.15 (9% discount — deeper than gold's 7%)
```

**Entry Rules:**

| Parameter | Rule | Rationale |
|-----------|------|-----------|
| **Size** | **50–75 shares** (NOT 100) | Higher volatility demands smaller position. 75 shares = $4,400 at $60. |
| **Order Type** | Limit order, resting | Never chase; patience even more critical for volatile asset |
| **Duration** | 7–10 days | Shorter than gold (more liquid, more responsive) |
| **Fill Confidence** | 30–40% expected | Deeper discount; lower fill rate expected |
| **Standing Sell** | Place immediately at base case PT | Locks profit (more critical for volatile positions) |
| **Invalidation** | Cancel if ISM drops below 50 OR copper drops 3%+ | Industrial demand breaking |

**Standing Sell Order (Automatic Exit):**

```
50% of position exits at: Base Case Price Target + 15–18%
Reason: Smaller position + higher volatility = need higher % gains to justify risk
Example: Layer 1 $60 + 17% = $70.20 standing sell (vs. 14% for gold)
```

**Why Smaller Size?**
- Silver miner volatility = 2.5–3.0x leverage to silver price
- A 50-share position at $60 = $3,000 (vs. 100 shares gold = $5,500)
- This caps downside in violent reversals while maintaining meaningful upside

## 5. SILVER MINER SPECIFIC RISK FACTORS

### Production & Cost Risks (Different from Gold)
- **Leverage to Silver Price:** AISC is critical. If silver price drops but costs don't, margin compression is violent.
  - Monitor: Cost per ounce of silver vs. silver price trend
  - Warning: If AISC growing faster than silver price, exit on any rally
- **Industrial Byproduct Risk:** Miners producing silver as byproduct (EXK, HL) = demand-sensitive
  - If primary metal (copper, zinc) mines shut, silver supply shrinks (positive)
  - But if auto/solar collapse, silver byproduct output stays high = supply glut
- **Silver Grade Degradation:** Unlike gold, if grade deteriorates, byproduct silver volume drops faster

### Macro & Commodity Risks (VERY Different from Gold)
- **Industrial Cycle is Primary:** ISM, PMI, auto production data matter MORE than gold for silver
  - ISM break below 50 = immediate 5–10% silver correction risk
  - This happens faster than gold macro shifts
- **Copper Correlation:** High (0.7–0.8). Copper weak = industrial demand weak = silver miners suffer
- **Base Metals Weakness:** If zinc, nickel, copper all selling, silver follows (not a safe haven in this scenario)
- **Demand Shock Risk:** Photography digital transition, solar panel supply gluts, battery tech shifts = silver demand risk (gold has no equivalent risk)

### Central Bank & Inflation Risks
- **Real Yields:** Less important than gold, but still matters
  - Rising real yields hit silver harder because industrial demand is demand-elastic
  - Falling yields help only if growth strong (not just inflation fears)
- **Fed Policy:** More about growth trajectory than rate levels
  - Hawkish pause (growth OK, controlling inflation) = **Good for silver** (industrial demand intact)
  - Hawkish hike (growth concerns, recession risk) = **Bad for silver** (demand destruction)
