## What Must Go Right

- Power prices: Electricity prices hold or rise — driven by AI demand, retirements of coal/nuclear, LNG exports, weather
- PPA wins: Additional data center / hyperscaler PPAs announced at favorable pricing ($80–120/MWh range is strong)
- Fleet reliability: Nuclear capacity factor stays above 90%; no major unplanned outages
- License extensions: NRC grants license renewals for aging nuclear plants (adds decades of cash flow)
- Capacity market tightening: PJM / ERCOT capacity prices rise as supply shrinks and demand grows
- Regulatory support: IRA nuclear production tax credit ($15/MWh) maintained or expanded

## Key Risks

- Power price collapse: Oversupply from renewables or demand miss pushes spot prices down; hedges eventually expire
- Nuclear unplanned outage: Unexpected shutdown = lost revenue + emergency power procurement cost
- NRC regulatory risk: License renewal denial or safety-related shutdown order
- PPA pricing disappointment: PPAs signed at lower-than-expected $/MWh or short duration
- Hyperscaler capex slowdown: Reduces demand for new AI-dedicated nuclear PPAs
- Rate case / regulatory risk: For regulated utility subsidiaries, regulators may not approve cost recovery
- IRA PTC risk: Nuclear production tax credit could be modified or repealed
