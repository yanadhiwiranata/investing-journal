## What Must Go Right

- Hyperscaler capex: Continued or accelerating DC buildout from MSFT, AMZN, GOOGL, META drives order flow with 6–12 month lag
- Backlog conversion: Orders in backlog convert to revenue on schedule without project delays or cancellations
- Margin expansion: Pricing holds or improves while input costs (copper, aluminum, steel) stabilize
- AI mix shift: Higher-margin liquid cooling and AI-specific power products grow as % of revenue
- Execution: No supply chain disruptions, manufacturing bottlenecks, or delivery failures

## Key Risks

- Hyperscaler capex pause or slowdown: The primary demand driver; any pullback flows through with a lag
- Input cost inflation: Copper, aluminum, transformer steel — raw material spikes compress margin
- Competition: Schneider Electric (European, not listed) is the market leader; Legrand, ABB also compete
- Project delays: Customer construction delays push revenue recognition out without reducing backlog
- Concentration risk: If top 3–5 hyperscaler customers slow orders simultaneously, growth stalls
- Execution risk: Rapid scaling of manufacturing capacity can cause quality or delivery issues
