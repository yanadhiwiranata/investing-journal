### Credit Risk
- **NPL Cycle:** Credit quality deterioration in [CRE / consumer / SME / UMKM] exposure
- **Provision Spike:** Forward-looking provisioning (CECL US; PSAK 71 Indonesia) front-loads losses
- **Sector Concentration Risk:** [Property exposure; commodity-linked loans; government programs]

### Interest Rate Risk
- **NIM Compression:** If asset-sensitive bank faces rate cuts faster than expected
- **Securities Portfolio:** HTM unrealized losses (US); repricing mismatch (Indonesia IDR/FCY)
- **Funding Cost Pressure:** Deposit pricing competition; CASA erosion

### Capital & Regulatory Risk
- **Capital Adequacy:** Below-buffer CET1/CAR triggers constraints on dividends and buybacks
- **Regulatory Change:** New Basel IV requirements (US); OJK POJK updates (Indonesia); dividend payout limits
- **Stress Test / DFAST (US):** Adverse scenario capital requirements may limit buybacks

### Macro Risk
- **Recession / Slowdown:** Loan demand drops; credit costs rise; fee income falls
- **Currency Risk (Indonesia):** IDR weakness raises foreign-currency loan stress; BBRI/BBCA USD exposure
- **Rate Path Uncertainty:** Faster-than-expected rate cuts (US) or BI rate hold/cut (Indonesia)

### Operational / Structural
- **Deposit Flight:** Post-SVB-style rate shopping; CASA ratio erosion
- **Digital Competition:** Fintech and neobanks capturing retail deposits and unsecured lending
- **Management / Governance:** Key executive departure; related-party risk (Indonesia SOE banks: BBRI, BMRI, BNI, BTN)
