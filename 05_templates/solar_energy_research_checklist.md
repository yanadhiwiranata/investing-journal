# Solar Energy Research Checklist

Use this checklist when analyzing solar energy companies. Organize by priority tier for efficient quarterly reviews.

---

## TIER 1: Critical Operating Metrics (Check Every Quarter)

### Shipments & Volume Trend
- [ ] Total shipments / MW sold (QoQ & YoY)
- [ ] Shipments by product line (modules, inverters, downstream MW, etc.)
- [ ] Shipments by end market (utility %, commercial %, residential %)
- [ ] Shipments by geography (US %, International %)
- [ ] Shipment guidance vs. actual (achievement rate)
- [ ] **Benchmark:** Beat or match guidance >80% of time
- [ ] **Benchmark:** QoQ growth consistent or accelerating; red flag if >10% QoQ decline
- [ ] Volume guide for next quarter vs. sequential build? (demand visibility)

### Average Selling Price (ASP) & Mix Quality
- [ ] ASP per unit or per watt (QoQ & YoY)
- [ ] ASP trend: improving, stable, or declining?
- [ ] Product mix shift impact on blended ASP (premium vs. commodity products)
- [ ] ASP by geography (US vs. international pricing power)
- [ ] **Benchmark:** ASP stable or improving; declining >5% QoQ = margin pressure signal
- [ ] Mix quality: Premium product % vs. commodity product %?
- [ ] Input cost inflation vs. ASP pass-through: Are margins compressing despite volume?

### Gross Margin & Profitability
- [ ] Gross margin % (QoQ & YoY)
- [ ] Operating margin % (QoQ & YoY)
- [ ] Margin bridge: What drove QoQ/YoY change? (ASP, mix, input costs, tariffs, fixed cost leverage)
- [ ] **Benchmark:** Gross margin >15% for manufacturers; >25% for downstream; red flag if <10%
- [ ] **Benchmark:** Margin stability; >300 bps QoQ decline = structural deterioration
- [ ] Fixed cost as % of revenue (higher = more leverage on volume growth)
- [ ] Tariff or subsidy impact quantified separately: is margin "real" or policy-supported?

### Capacity, Utilization & Expansion Status
- [ ] Nameplate capacity (MW or units) - current and by facility
- [ ] Actual run-rate capacity utilization (% of nameplate)
- [ ] Capacity utilization trend: improving toward target or declining?
- [ ] **Benchmark:** Target utilization >85%; <70% = underutilized / demand weakness signal
- [ ] Planned capacity additions (MW, capex $, completion date)
- [ ] Ramp plan for new capacity: timeline to meaningful utilization
- [ ] **Benchmark:** New capacity should reach >60% utilization within 6 months of start
- [ ] Manufacturing yield or conversion rate (% of inputs converted to sellable product)
- [ ] Yield trend: improving, stable, or deteriorating? (impacts effective capacity)

### Backlog, Pipeline & Order Visibility
- [ ] Total backlog (units/MW) and backlog as months of supply
- [ ] Backlog by end market (utility-scale, commercial, residential %)
- [ ] Order-to-shipment conversion rate (% of backlog shipped each quarter)
- [ ] **Benchmark:** Backlog = 2+ months forward visibility; <1 month = demand risk
- [ ] **Benchmark:** Conversion rate >90%; <70% = execution or delivery bottleneck
- [ ] New bookings / order intake (quarterly trend)
- [ ] Customer concentration in backlog (top 3 customers % of backlog)
- [ ] Any contract cancellations or pushouts (early warning of demand deterioration)

---

## TIER 2: Financial Health (Check Every Quarter)

### Balance Sheet & Liquidity
- [ ] Cash on hand (absolute amount & in months of operating expense)
- [ ] Total debt outstanding and maturity schedule
- [ ] Undrawn credit facilities and covenant status
- [ ] Net cash / net debt position
- [ ] **Benchmark:** Cash >2 months operating expense
- [ ] **Benchmark:** Total debt / EBITDA <2.5x; >3.5x = leverage risk
- [ ] Working capital trend: is inventory or receivables growing faster than sales? (cash trap)

### Cash Generation & Capital Allocation
- [ ] Operating cash flow (OCF) vs. prior quarter (trend)
- [ ] Free cash flow (FCF) = OCF − capex (is company self-funding growth?)
- [ ] **Benchmark:** Positive FCF; company should not need to raise capital
- [ ] Capex spending (as % of revenue): expansion capex vs. maintenance capex
- [ ] Growth capex: planned annual capex through 2028 (capacity expansion roadmap)
- [ ] Dividend or buyback activity: size, payout ratio, sustainability
- [ ] Share dilution: new shares issued for employees, M&A, or capital raises

### Funding & Dilution Risk
- [ ] Any outstanding convertibles, warrants, or options (fully diluted share count)
- [ ] Recent or planned capital raises (equity, debt, asset sales)
- [ ] Financing needs for capacity expansion: what's the capex plan vs. internal cash generation?
- [ ] **Benchmark:** Company should fund growth through FCF; equity raises = value dilution risk
- [ ] Covenant compliance (if debt): any covenants approaching breach risk?

---

## TIER 3: Growth & Execution (Check Quarterly; Deep Dive 2x Annually)

### Capacity Expansion & Project Pipeline
- [ ] Major capacity expansion projects (list by facility name, type, size)
- [ ] Expected production start date for each new facility
- [ ] Capex budget per MW and any overruns / revisions vs. original estimate
- [ ] Permitting, environmental, or grid interconnection status for each facility
- [ ] Execution confidence: on-time / at-risk / behind schedule?
- [ ] **Benchmark:** Capex per MW efficiency; >20% overrun or >6 months delay = execution issue
- [ ] Post-expansion utilization ramp plan and timeline to target

### Guidance & Outlook
- [ ] Full-year shipment guidance (updated? raised/lowered? by how much?)
- [ ] ASP guidance (updated? trend direction?)
- [ ] Margin guidance for current year & forward outlook (2-year view)
- [ ] Capex guidance for current year & next 2 years (capacity expansion roadmap)
- [ ] Management tone on confidence (positive / cautious / concerned)
- [ ] Guidance track record: beat, miss, or match (last 4 quarters)
- [ ] **Benchmark:** Guidance beat or matched >75% of time; consistent misses = credibility issue
- [ ] Any guidance revisions this quarter: direction and magnitude

### Product Development & Innovation
- [ ] New products launched or in development pipeline (cells, modules, inverters, storage)
- [ ] Competitive positioning: efficiency, cost, or feature advantage?
- [ ] Tariff exposure if manufacturing outside US (Section 45X / IRA compliance plan)
- [ ] R&D spend as % of revenue and trend (innovation investment rate)

---

## TIER 4: Risk & External Factors (Check Quarterly + Ad Hoc)

### Policy, Subsidy & Tariff Exposure
- [ ] IRA / Section 45X eligibility: % of revenue benefiting from tax credit
- [ ] Domestic content requirement status: % of COGS sourced domestically
- [ ] Tariff exposure: current rates on silicon, polysilicon, cells, modules, inverters (by import source)
- [ ] Tariff phase-in or expiration dates (any scheduled changes ahead?)
- [ ] Impact quantified: tariff cost per unit or % of margin
- [ ] Hedging strategy: sourcing diversification, tariff pass-through to customers
- [ ] **Benchmark:** >30% of margin supported by subsidies = policy/termination risk; <10% = safer
- [ ] ITC / investment tax credit demand: residential, commercial, utility-scale mix
- [ ] **Red flag:** Any announced tariff increases, quota additions, or subsidy expirations in next 12 months?

### End-Market Demand & Customer Risk
- [ ] Utility-scale solar pipeline: GW of awarded projects by developer (visibility to backlog fill)
- [ ] Residential solar demand: mortgage rates, customer acquisition cost, conversion rates
- [ ] Commercial solar adoption: corporate renewable commitments, payback period
- [ ] Geographic demand mix: California, Texas, other high-growth states; international
- [ ] Customer concentration: top 5 customers as % of revenue (>20% = concentration risk)
- [ ] Major customer wins/losses or contract changes (early signal of demand shift)
- [ ] **Red flag:** >30% revenue from top 3 customers or single-customer concentration

### Input Cost & Supply Chain
- [ ] Silicon / polysilicon spot price trend and outlook
- [ ] Aluminum, copper, glass, inverter component pricing
- [ ] Labor cost inflation (manufacturing wage trends)
- [ ] Supply chain bottlenecks: container costs, logistics, grid interconnection delays
- [ ] Sourcing diversification: single-source vs. multi-source for critical inputs
- [ ] Long-term supply contracts vs. spot market exposure
- [ ] **Red flag:** >25% of COGS exposed to spot prices or single supplier

### Competitive Positioning & Market Share
- [ ] Market share in core product/market (modules %, inverters %, downstream MW %)
- [ ] Market share trend: gaining / stable / losing (YoY comparison)
- [ ] Competitive differentiation: cost leader / technology leader / service leader?
- [ ] Major competitors and their capacity additions (industry supply-demand balance)
- [ ] **Red flag:** Losing market share while competitors expand capacity = demand or pricing pressure

### Jurisdiction & Regulatory
- [ ] Primary manufacturing jurisdictions: US / Vietnam / China / India / other
- [ ] IRA domestic content rules impacting sourcing decisions
- [ ] China tariffs or trade tension impact (if relevant)
- [ ] State-level policies: net metering, interconnection rules, RPS targets
- [ ] Grid interconnection bottlenecks (can projects reach market on time?)
- [ ] **Red flag:** New tariffs, permitting delays, or policy changes mid-quarter

---

## Final Judgment (After Each Earnings)

- [ ] **Volume Status:** On track / Ahead / Behind guidance
  - Evidence: [shipment vs. forecast, revised full-year outlook]

- [ ] **ASP & Margin Status:** Improving / Stable / Deteriorating
  - Evidence: [QoQ/YoY trend, mix impact, cost bridge]

- [ ] **Capacity & Execution:** On schedule / Ahead / Behind
  - Evidence: [utilization progress, project timeline, capex tracking]

- [ ] **Balance Sheet:** Strong enough for planned capex? Yes / Marginal / Needs capital raise
  - Evidence: [cash position, FCF, debt maturity, project funding]

- [ ] **Thesis Validation:** Bull case strengthened / Unchanged / Weakened
  - Evidence: [execution vs. thesis, guidance change, competitive position shift]

- [ ] **Policy / Subsidy Risk:** Stable / At risk / Critical
  - Evidence: [tariff exposure, IRA eligibility, subsidy dependency changes]

- [ ] **Next Critical Data:** What single metric or event matters most next quarter?
  - Answer: [e.g., "capacity ramp timing", "ASP deterioration signal", "tariff announcement"]

- [ ] **Recommended Action:** Buy / Hold / Reduce / Sell
  - Rationale: [conviction and time horizon]

---

## Analyst-Specific Priority Weight (Customize as Needed)

**Most Important (60% of conviction):**
- Shipment growth and guidance credibility (beat/miss rate)
- ASP and margin trajectory (subsidy vs. structural support)

**Secondary (25% of conviction):**
- Capacity utilization and expansion execution
- Free cash flow and balance sheet strength

**Tertiary (15% of conviction):**
- Market share trends
- Analyst sentiment shifts
- Policy/tariff near-term impact
