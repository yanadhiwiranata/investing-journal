# Power & Cooling Infrastructure Research Checklist

Use this checklist for data center power, thermal management, and electrical equipment names: VRT, ETN, MOD, HUBB, NVT.

These companies sit between industrial and technology. Their earnings follow hyperscaler capex with a 6–12 month lag, so always read MSFT/AMZN/GOOGL capex commentary before forming a view.

---

## 1. Classify The Demand Driver

- Is the company's AI/DC growth driven by new hyperscaler buildouts, colo expansions, or enterprise upgrades?
- What % of revenue is AI data center vs. legacy industrial / telecom?
- Is the company more exposed to power delivery, cooling, or electrical distribution?
- Which hyperscalers or colo operators are the named or implied customers?

## 2. Backlog And Order Quality

- What is the current book-to-bill ratio? (>1.0 = healthy, <1.0 = demand softening)
- How large is the total backlog in dollars, and how many quarters does it cover?
- Is backlog growing faster than revenue (good) or slower (orders slowing)?
- Are orders concentrated in a few large projects or diversified?
- What are lead times? Lengthening = strong demand; shortening = normalization or softening

## 3. Margin Quality

- Is gross margin expanding (pricing power + scale) or compressing (input costs + mix)?
- What is the AI/DC segment margin vs. the legacy industrial margin?
- Are raw material costs (copper, aluminum, transformer steel) a headwind or tailwind?
- Is the company passing through input cost increases in pricing?
- Is operating leverage visible — does revenue growth translate to faster earnings growth?

## 4. Competitive Position

- How does market share compare to Schneider Electric (private, ~30–35% share)?
- Is the company winning new DC programs or defending existing ones?
- Are competitors cutting prices or lengthening lead times (industry tightness)?
- Does the company have liquid cooling or advanced thermal products for next-gen AI racks?

## 5. Hyperscaler Capex As Leading Indicator

Read these earnings calls before any VRT/ETN analysis — they are the demand signal:
- Microsoft (MSFT): Azure AI capex guidance
- Amazon (AMZN): AWS infrastructure spend
- Alphabet (GOOGL): GCP and TPU buildout
- Meta (META): AI infrastructure capex
- Any colo operator (EQIX, DLR): DC expansion pipeline

Ask: Is hyperscaler capex guidance up, flat, or down? If up → order pipeline for VRT/ETN strengthens 6–12 months out. If down → watch backlog for slippage.

## 6. Balance Sheet And Capital Allocation

- Net debt level and leverage ratio — industrials can carry more leverage but watch interest coverage
- Acquisition strategy — are they buying product capabilities or just revenue?
- Buyback or dividend — signals management confidence
- Capex intensity for manufacturing expansion — are they investing ahead of demand?

## 7. Execution And Management Credibility

- Did management's book-to-bill commentary last quarter match the actual reported number?
- Are they hitting delivery timelines or citing supply chain issues?
- Is organic growth running ahead of or behind guidance?
- How has gross margin tracked vs. what management said at the start of the year?

## 8. Final Judgment

- Is the book-to-bill above 1.0 with a growing backlog? (Most important single check)
- Is AI/DC revenue growing faster than the company overall?
- Is hyperscaler capex guidance supportive for the next 2–3 quarters?
- Are margins expanding or at least stable despite cost pressures?
- What is the entry timing — are we in a hyperscaler capex acceleration cycle or digestion phase?
- What would invalidate the thesis quickly? (Book-to-bill below 1.0 for two quarters, or major hyperscaler capex cut)
